Interface-Aware PINN Notes

Non-exhaustive personal study notes summarizing interface-related PINN approaches reported in the literature. These notes are intended for background understanding and do not represent original contributions or a complete survey.

1. Domain-Decomposed PINNs

cPINN (conservative PINN)

  • Splits the domain into subdomains and enforces flux continuity across interfaces.
  • Encodes conservation laws explicitly through interface constraints.
  • Often implemented with multiple networks trained in parallel.

XPINN

  • General domain decomposition with separate neural networks per subdomain.
  • Subdomains are coupled through interface loss terms.
  • Commonly used for problems with non-smooth solutions or heterogeneous coefficients.

FBPINN

  • Uses overlapping subdomains combined via windowing or partition-of-unity ideas.
  • Overlaps help soften interface stitching compared to hard constraints.
  • Reported to scale better for larger or more complex domains.

2. Multi-Network Formulations for Non-Smooth PDEs

Multi-domain PINN (M-PINN)

  • Trains separate networks on different regions of the domain.
  • Primarily used for forward problems with piecewise-smooth solutions.
  • Interface conditions are imposed explicitly during training.

DS-PINN / nDS-PINN

  • Introduces interface continuity conditions with spatial separation strategies.
  • Includes normalization techniques to balance loss terms across regions.
  • Often applied to diffusion-type problems with material interfaces.

3. Interface-Aware Representations

Discontinuity and cusp-aware neural representations

  • Modify the neural representation to better express jumps or derivative cusps.
  • May incorporate level-set or piecewise features into the network input.
  • Typically problem-dependent in how interface information is encoded.

4. Variational and Least-Squares Neural Solvers

Mesh-free variational deep learning approaches

  • Reformulate interface PDEs using variational or least-squares objectives.
  • Neural networks approximate trial functions instead of enforcing strong residuals.
  • Interface effects are handled through weak formulations or additional terms.

Piecewise neural solvers with adaptive sampling

  • Employ multiple networks with least-squares objectives.
  • Sampling density may be adjusted based on residual magnitude.
  • Often used to allocate more samples near interfaces.

5. Adaptive and Meta Strategies

Loss-balancing and interfaced neural network methods

  • Introduce strategies to balance competing loss terms across domains.
  • May treat interface enforcement as a multi-objective optimization problem.

Meta-learning interface conditions

  • Explores learning or selecting interface constraints automatically.
  • Typically relies on families of related problems for training.
  • Proposed as a way to reduce manual tuning of interface conditions.
